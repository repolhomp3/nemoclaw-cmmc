## 1. Purpose

This matrix defines proposed retention, review, and disposition rules for data created, stored, or processed by NemoClaw on the DGX Spark platform.

It is intended to reduce two common readiness failures:
- retaining sensitive derived CUI indefinitely with no owner or rationale
- deleting evidence too early to support incident response, assessment, or audit review

---

## 2. Scope and Interpretation

This document applies to source CUI, derived CUI, operational records, and evidence generated by the NemoClaw system.

**Important:** Unless an approved enterprise records schedule requires a longer period, the values below should be treated as the **proposed minimum default retention targets for this system**. If enterprise policy, contract requirements, litigation hold, incident handling, or assessment needs require longer retention, the stricter requirement governs.

## 4. Disposition Rules

### 4.1 General rule
When data reaches the end of its approved retention period and no hold applies, it shall be:
- securely deleted from active storage
- removed from export or temporary locations
- allowed to age out of backups according to backup retention
- sanitized or destroyed on removable media in accordance with approved media procedures

### 4.2 Holds and exceptions
Do **not** dispose of data if any of the following apply:
- incident investigation or evidence preservation hold
- legal hold
- contractual reporting or audit need
- active POA&M, assessment, or remediation need
- formally approved exception by records/compliance owner

### 4.3 Ownership
Each data class shall have an identified owner responsible for:
- approving retention value
- approving exceptions
- confirming disposition trigger
- preserving records where required

## 5. Review Requirements

This matrix should be reviewed:
- before production accreditation or boundary entry
- whenever new data classes are created
- whenever export, logging, or model handling changes
- at least annually
- after any incident that reveals uncontrolled copies of derived CUI

---

## 6. Assessment Focus Areas

Assessors and internal reviewers should verify that:
- derived CUI stores are all included in this matrix
- retention values are approved, not just proposed indefinitely
- backup retention does not undermine deletion/disposition intent
- export locations are not becoming permanent unmanaged records stores
- temporary caches are actually purged on schedule

## 7. Mapping to NIST SP 800-171 Rev. 2 / CMMC L2

This matrix primarily supports:
- **AU.L2-3.3.1** create and retain audit records
- **AC.L2-3.1.3** control CUI flow
- **MP.L2-3.8.1** protect media containing CUI
- **MP.L2-3.8.2** limit access to CUI on media
- **MP.L2-3.8.3** sanitize media before disposal or reuse
- **SC.L2-3.13.16** protect confidentiality of CUI at rest
- **CM.L2-3.4.1** maintain baseline understanding of stored data and records

---

## 8. NemoClaw-Specific Notes

For this system, the retention review should specifically challenge any assumption that the following can be kept forever without owner approval:
- chat history
- generated summaries
- embeddings
- exported assessor bundles
- copied intake artifacts
- temporary prompt/context caches

Those are exactly the places where AI systems quietly expand the CUI footprint if no one is paying attention.
